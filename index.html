As part of my graduating project, I work collaboratively in a group to design a CPR aid device that provides live feedback on the CPR performance so users can adjust and improve their procedure. My main task includes microcontroller coding (specifically Arduino Uno and Arduino Pro Mini), prototype design using Solidworks, experimental design and reporting. I am also responsible for MATLAB coding to acquire and analyze experimental data.

In summer of 2019, I participated in the internship program BSUITE hosted by Keck Graduate Institute. The bioprocessing training program that aims to familiarize interns with quality assurance, process development and related attributes of monoclonal antibodies production. I was familiarized with the procedure of aseptic techniques used for mammalian cells perfusion as well as some of the common machines often used to monitor the viability of the cells such as the NOVA, ViCell and Osmometer. The program also encourage interns to learn about FDA regulations, manufacturing guidelines, quality control and quality assurance process involved in biopharmaceutical production.

I am currently working as a student worker at the University of California, Riverside Library. I interact mainly with the OCLC system and ALMA system to search and catalog Vietnamese books that the library receive from oversees.
